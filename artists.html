Don Pablo were born out of the whimsy that followed the beginning of a new millennium. Neither in or out of fashion their purple patch, between 2002 and 2006, produced a collection of wonderfully imaginative song.

Now long since buried beneath the weight of responsibility and service delivery the duo are no longer producing in this era of content creation, but 8 Meg Stick Records are committed to unearthing the treasure trove that is Don Pablo’s output and making it available to literally anyone that will listen.

A little-known fact is that 8 Meg Stick is the title for a Don Pablo song.

Their sound is as organic as it is plastic and as warm as it is cold. Hammond organs tremble on their debut release Campfire and synths growl on their second single Pretty Picture with the latter’s Bonny & Clyde evoking prose setting a desperate scene with huge imagination. But it is with the release of the E.P. Much Better in Our Youth that the real identity of Don Pablo comes to the fore with its melancholy tales of lost loves, missed opportunities and the weather.

If you are looking for the dark matter that holds 8 Meg Stick together, let’s face it who isn’t? Then look no further than PAULI. One fifth We Used To See The Sky, the Pablo in Don Pablo and who Pengamax uses when they outsource production duties PAULI is 8 Meg Stick Records (no actually is).

2020 was the year PAULI found his words and voice. The state of his mind has been documented through the year and we’ll get a chance to peek inside throughout 2021. It also the year he discovered the joys of open tuning and single figure guitar chords unlocking an entire audio pallet that was previously beyond him…

So on the 22nd of January with the debut release 4 Walls we remember those early moments of lockdown when the weather was warm, Zoom was a computer virus and it all felt like that bit between Christmas and New Year when you just eat and drink because you’re too drunk and full to leave the house.

Not all things are created equal. And that is true of the curious beast and multi-instrumentalist that is Pengamax.

With a veritable avalanche of output waiting on the 8 Meg Stick drive we can confidentially state the Pengamax’s desire to shape shift around the musical spectrum is in full throttle.

First single Mitti is a confident stride in the world of blues. It is a deeply affecting piece of storytelling that is modern, contemporary and resonates against pandemic times. But there is no resting on the laurels of the well-trodden virtuoso guitar playing path and the second release sees Pengamax in widescreen cinematic mood. Ethereal and epic soundscapes morph through time and space and evoke scenes of a sunrise over a world yet known on their E.P. G Formations.

And what will be next? Your guess is as good as mine…

Bristol five Piece We Used To See The Sky owe their existence to Damon Albarn and their name to British Romanticism painter Samuel Palmer. Let’s start with more famous former (unless of course you are a keen observer of the nineteenth century art scene). We Used To See The Sky were formed from the ashes of a non-starting Damon Albarn tribute act after a conversation in an empty carpark where keyboard player Paul and singer Mike agreed that starting an originals band was the future. Steve on guitar also migrated across from the tribute act Allbarn (geddit?) and so We Used To See The Sky became so.

Regulars on the Bristol live circuit pre-pandemic the 5 piece, which also includes Holly on bass and the other Steve on drums, had become a real favourite of venues like the Thunderbolt and the Louisiana.

Their non-linier musical style fits in well with the 8 Meg Stick ethos that anything goes. Debut track, Slippy Guppy (yep actually is) offers a warm radiance of acoustic guitars reverberating through you being whilst second track Deep Fake is built on a mass of big beats and articulates the modern mistrust of technology.

As for Samuel Palmer, you need to see We Used To See The Sky live where keyboard player Paul likes to conduct a lecture on the subject usually when the band are tuning down.
